The System

What Goes On Your Walls.

Burncross, Sheffield — EWI installation in progress. Preparation determines performance.

A properly installed EWI system follows 7 stages. Every one matters — the performance of the thermal envelope depends on the quality of each layer.

Book Survey ↗
01 Survey & Thermal Assessment
›

Detailed building survey to assess structural condition, moisture levels, and thermal requirements. Thermal modelling determines the insulation thickness needed to achieve target U-values. We'll advise the right system and thickness before any commitment is made.

02 Preparation & Scaffolding
›

Professional scaffold erected for safe access to all elevations. Exterior surfaces cleaned, all defects repaired, damp-proof course checked and protected. The condition of the substrate is critical — we never apply EWI over a failing wall surface.

03 Insulation Board Installation
›

EPS, PUR, or Mineral Wool boards are mechanically fixed and adhesive-bonded to the external walls. Boards are tightly butted, level, and aligned across all elevations. No gaps are permitted — a single thermal bridge undermines the performance of the entire system.

04 Joint Sealing & Edge Profiles
›

All board joints, corners, window reveals, and pipe penetrations are sealed and fitted with professional edge profiles. This eliminates thermal bridges — the main cause of cold spots, condensation, and mould in buildings with inadequate insulation.

05 Reinforced Base Coat
›

Polymer-modified base coat applied over the full insulation layer with embedded alkali-resistant fibreglass mesh pressed in while wet. This creates a crack-resistant, structurally sound foundation for the finish coat and provides the system's long-term durability.

06 Finish Render Application
›

Silicone render, monocouche, or acrylic finish applied in your chosen colour and texture. Silicone is our recommendation — hydrophobic, self-cleaning, and through-coloured for 25+ years without repainting. The finish transforms the appearance of the property while protecting the insulation system from weather.

07 Quality Inspection & Certification
›

Full quality inspection of all elevations before sign-off. Manufacturer and workmanship warranty documentation provided. Zero deposit throughout — you pay only when the work is complete and you're fully satisfied.

0.040 W/mK
EPS — Expanded Polystyrene ★ Recommended
›

The most popular and cost-effective EWI system — proven 25+ year performance across thousands of UK properties. Excellent thermal performance at 0.040 W/mK, lightweight, and easy to work with on residential builds. Our primary recommendation for most Sheffield homes. Typical installed cost: £40–£60/m².

0.024 W/mK
PUR — Polyurethane Premium Performance
›

The best thermal performance available at 0.023–0.025 W/mK — meaning thinner boards achieve the same U-value as thicker EPS. Ideal where board thickness is a constraint (narrow reveals, planning restrictions) or where maximum thermal performance is needed. Typical installed cost: £70–£90/m².

0.038 W/mK
Mineral Wool Historic Buildings
›

Natural mineral fibre with A1 fire rating and excellent vapour permeability — the specialist choice for listed buildings, properties in conservation areas, and older solid-wall construction where breathability is essential. Often the only EWI system approved for historic properties.

01Product
What is external wall insulation (EWI)?

EWI wraps your property in insulation boards (EPS, PUR or Mineral Wool), finished with a protective render coat. It creates a continuous thermal envelope that eliminates cold spots, damp, and high heating bills. Unlike internal insulation, it doesn't reduce room sizes and doesn't require you to vacate during installation.

05Systems
What's the difference between EPS, PUR and Mineral Wool EWI?

EPS (0.040 W/mK) is the most cost-effective at £40–£60/m² — our recommendation for most properties. PUR (0.023–0.025 W/mK) offers the best performance at £70–£90/m², ideal where board thickness is a constraint. Mineral Wool is the specialist choice for listed buildings — breathable, A1 fire rated, and often the only system approved for historic properties.

Can EWI be fitted to any Sheffield property?

EWI works on most masonry — brick, blockwork, stone, aerated concrete. Listed buildings may have restrictions, but breathable Mineral Wool systems are often approved. Do I need planning permission for EWI in Sheffield?

In most cases EWI falls under permitted development — no planning permission required. Exceptions include listed buildings, conservation areas, and some flats. We check the planning position at the free site survey so you know exactly where you stand before committing to anything.
