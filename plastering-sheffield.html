How We Work

How We Plaster Your Walls.

Sheffield — dry lining installation in progress. Preparation is what determines the finish.

A professional plaster finish follows 6 stages — each one matters. Cutting corners at any stage shows up in the finished result.

Book a Visit ↗
01 Survey & Assessment
›

We assess the substrate — walls and ceiling — for moisture, hollow sections, previous repairs, and structural movement. The right plaster system is specified for the surface type. We advise honestly whether a skim or full replaster is the right solution before any commitment is made.

02 Surface Preparation
›

Loose and hollow plaster is removed. Surfaces are cleaned to a sound substrate. Masonry is dampened and PVA bonded to control suction. Angle beads and stop beads are fixed to all external angles. Good preparation is the single biggest factor in whether the finished plaster stays on.

03 First Coat Application
›

For traditional two-coat work, a bonding or scratch coat is applied to build up the level and create a mechanical key for the finish coat. On plasterboard walls, the boarding is taped and jointed at this stage. The first coat sets the foundation — the finish coat can only be as flat as the undercoat beneath it.

04 Ruling Off & Levelling
›

Once the first coat has set sufficiently, it is ruled off with a straight edge to produce a flat, level surface across the full wall or ceiling. High and low spots are identified and corrected. Getting the levels right at this stage is critical — it determines whether the final finish reads as truly flat.

05 Finish Skim Coat
›

A thin finish plaster (2-3mm) is applied in two passes — laid on and then trowelled back to a perfectly smooth, hard finish. The plasterer works the surface until completely flat, tight, and free of trowel marks. This coat is what you see after decoration. It is the most skilled part of the job and where the difference between good and great plastering is decided.

06 Curing & Hand-Over
›

We advise on drying conditions to prevent cracking — typically 3-5 days before mist coat. A full quality inspection with the client before sign-off. Five-year workmanship guarantee provided. Zero deposit throughout — you pay only when the work is complete and you're fully satisfied.

Discuss Options ↗
2-coat System
Traditional Two-Coat Plaster ★ Recommended for Masonry
›

The gold standard for solid masonry walls — brick, block, and stone. A bonding or scratch coat builds up the level; a finish skim coat creates the smooth surface. Traditional two-coat plaster is the most durable system and the best choice for Sheffield's older solid-wall properties. Typical room cost: £250–£600 depending on size and prep.

Board System
Dry Lining & Plasterboard Best for New Build & Insulation
›

Plasterboard fixed to stud partitions or dot-and-dab applied to masonry walls, then taped, jointed and skimmed. Faster than traditional wet plastering and excellent for new build extensions, partition walls, and insulated wall upgrades. Insulated backing board (dot-and-dab) significantly improves the thermal performance of solid masonry walls — a common upgrade in Sheffield homes.

Bond System
Bonding & Skim Uneven & Mixed Surfaces
›

Bonding undercoat applied over exposed brick, block, or patchy existing surfaces to create an even, level base, then finish skimmed to a smooth surface. The most flexible approach — ideal for rooms with mixed substrate conditions (part old plaster, part masonry, part board), irregular surfaces, or where a sound existing plaster needs bringing to a consistent level before skimming.

Existing Surface Condition Biggest single variable
›

Sound, flat existing plaster that only needs a skim coat is the cheapest option. Hollow or crumbling plaster that needs hacking off and full re-doing takes significantly more time. Artex ceilings require mechanical removal before replastering. We assess the surface condition at quotation so there are no surprises.

Room Size & Height Area determines material and labour
›

Larger rooms take more time and material. High ceilings add complexity and stilts-work. Rooms with many doors, windows, or alcoves require more cutting and detailing. We measure precisely at quotation — our prices are based on actual area, not rough estimates.

Number of Coats Required Skim vs two-coat vs bonding
›

A single finish skim over sound plasterboard is the quickest and most cost-effective work we do. Traditional two-coat work (bonding then skim) takes longer per room. Rooms requiring a bonding or floating coat to level out an uneven substrate add time and material cost. We specify the right system and price accordingly.

Access & Site Conditions Can affect programme and cost
›

Occupied properties require furniture covering and protecting — factor in time for this. Properties mid-renovation where other trades are working are typically faster to plaster as rooms are cleared. Parking and material delivery access can also affect logistics on tight Sheffield terraced streets. We discuss site conditions at quotation.

How much does plastering cost in Sheffield? +

A standard room skim in Sheffield costs £200–£400 for walls only or £300–£600 for walls and ceiling, depending on size and surface condition. A full terraced house typically costs £1,500–£2,500 and a semi-detached £2,500–£4,500. Full renovation and new-build plastering starts from £3,000. We provide a free, fully itemised quotation with no obligation — zero deposit required.

How long does fresh plaster take to dry? +

Fresh plaster typically takes 3-5 days to dry enough for a mist coat (watered-down emulsion first coat), and up to 4-6 weeks to fully cure before applying standard emulsion. Opening windows and using gentle background heat helps speed drying — but avoid pointing direct fan heaters at fresh plaster as this causes cracking. We give you a clear drying programme on completion of every job.

Can you skim over existing plaster? +

Yes — if the existing plaster is sound (not hollow or crumbling), we can apply a fresh finish skim coat directly over it. We check the existing plaster first by tapping across the surface. Hollow sections are cut back and made good before skimming. The surface is PVA bonded to control suction. If the plaster is extensively hollow, a full replaster gives better long-term results — we'll advise honestly which is the right call.

What's the difference between skimming and plastering? +

Plastering is the full process — applying base coat(s) plus a finish coat to create a smooth surface. Skimming specifically means applying a thin (2-3mm) finish coat of plaster over an existing surface — usually plasterboard or sound existing plaster — to create a smooth result ready for decorating. Skimming is faster but only suitable where the existing surface is structurally sound. Full plastering involves a thicker base or bonding coat first, followed by the skim.

Can you patch plaster rather than replaster the whole room? +

Yes — we regularly carry out patch repairs for cracks, impact damage, damp repairs, and isolated hollow sections. Good patch work is harder than it looks: the repair needs to be feathered and blended so it's invisible under paint. We advise honestly whether a patch will give a good result or whether the surface condition means a full replaster is better value — which it often is if more than 30-40% of the plaster is hollow.

What is dry lining and when is it used? +

Dry lining means fixing plasterboard to walls or ceilings instead of applying wet plaster directly to masonry. It's the standard method for new-build stud partitions, insulated wall upgrades (dot-and-dab plasterboard with insulated backing board), and ceiling refits. Plasterboard is taped, jointed, and skimmed to a smooth finish. It's faster than traditional wet plastering and excellent for achieving level results on uneven masonry without the drying time of thick build-up coats.

Can you remove artex and replaster? +

Yes — artex removal and re-skim is one of our most requested services in Sheffield. We check whether the artex contains asbestos first (pre-1999 artex must be tested before work begins) and advise accordingly. Artex is either mechanically removed back to the substrate, or — where it is sound and stable — a float coat is applied over it before the finish skim. The result is a completely smooth, flat ceiling that transforms the room's appearance.

How long does plastering a room take? +

A standard single room skim (walls and ceiling) typically takes one day — most rooms are completed in 6-8 hours. Larger rooms, full replastering (base coat + skim), or artex removal typically take 1.5–2 days. A full house replaster is usually 3-5 working days. We provide a clear programme at quotation so you know what to expect before we start.

Do you plaster ceilings as well as walls? +

Yes — ceilings are a core part of our plastering work and are included in all room quotations as standard. We skim and replaster ceilings of all sizes including large open-plan areas. We also carry out ceiling repairs for cracks, water damage, sagging plasterboard, and artex removal. Ceiling plastering is included in the same room price — we don't charge separately for ceilings unless the scope is ceiling-only.

How do I know if my plaster needs replacing? +

Key signs that plaster needs replacing: hollow sounds when tapped (delamination), extensive cracking, bulging or bowing surfaces, persistent damp marks after the moisture source is fixed, or heavy previous patching that has left an uneven surface. Small isolated cracks are usually patchable. We give an honest assessment at quotation — we won't recommend replastering if a skim or patch will give an equally good result.

Do you plaster new build extensions and renovations? +

Yes — new build and extension plastering is a significant part of our work in Sheffield. We plaster plasterboard partitions, ceiling joists, block and brick masonry walls, and provide a complete plastered finish ready for first fix decoration. For extensions, we can match the finish and level of the existing plasterwork in adjacent rooms seamlessly. We coordinate with other trades and provide a firm programme to avoid hold-ups on site.
