What Affects Plastering Cost?

Two rooms of the same size can cost very different amounts to plaster. These are the factors our plasterers assess at the free site visit:

📒
Room Size & Surface Area

The total m² of walls and ceiling to be plastered is the primary cost driver. A large living room with a high ceiling costs more than a compact bedroom, even when both need the same type of work.

🧷
Coat Type

A skim coat over sound existing plaster is quick and affordable. A full strip-back and two-coat replaster takes longer, uses more material and costs more — but is necessary when the existing plaster has failed.

🛠
Surface Preparation Needed

Heavily contaminated walls, deep repairs, loose or blown plaster, and old tile adhesive all add preparation time before plastering can begin. Heavily patched walls may also need bonding coat first.

🚫
Access & Ceiling Height

Standard ceiling heights (2.4–2.7m) are straightforward. Rooms with higher ceilings, angled ceilings, dormer bedrooms or difficult access may require additional equipment and setup time.

🏭
Artex Removal

Artex ceilings and textured wall coatings require removal or encapsulation before skimming can take place. Artex applied before 1985 may contain asbestos, which requires specialist testing and removal — see the section below.

🔋
Number of Rooms

Plastering multiple rooms in one visit is more cost-efficient than separate single-room jobs. Mobilisation, site setup and material delivery are shared costs — so whole-house projects typically cost less per m² than one-room jobs.

Skim Coat vs Full Replaster — When Do You Need Each?

This is the most common question we get before booking a survey. The distinction matters both for cost and for the quality of the end result. Here is an honest guide:

Factor Skim Coat Full Replaster
When to use Existing plaster is solid, sound and bonded Existing plaster is failing, hollow, blown or damp-damaged
What happens 2–3mm finishing plaster applied over existing surface Existing plaster hacked off, walls prepared, two-coat system applied
Typical cost £8–12 / m² £12–18 / m²
Time per room 1 day for most rooms 1.5–2.5 days depending on size
Drying time 2–3 weeks before painting 3–6 weeks before painting (thicker coats)
Longevity 20–30+ years if base is sound 30–50+ years with correct application

The honest truth: if the existing plaster feels hollow when you tap it, or if you can see cracks, bubbling or staining, skimming over it will not fix the underlying problem. It may look good initially but is likely to fail within a few years. Our plasterers always advise honestly at the survey — we will never recommend a full replaster when a skim coat is sufficient.

Artex Removal & Replaster — Extra Costs

⚠️ Important: Artex Applied Before 1985 May Contain Asbestos

Artex was a widely used textured coating in Sheffield homes from the 1960s through to the early 1990s. Artex applied before 1985 frequently contains chrysotile (white) asbestos fibres, which are hazardous if disturbed. Before any artex removal, the material must be sampled and tested by a UKAS-accredited laboratory.

If asbestos is confirmed, removal must be carried out by a licensed asbestos contractor under controlled conditions — this is a legal requirement and a cost additional to the plastering work itself. We can advise you on the correct process and refer you to trusted local contractors for the removal phase.

If artex post-1985 (or confirmed asbestos-free artex) needs removing, we can skim directly over it in many cases, or remove and replaster where required.

📌 Artex removal & replaster: add approximately £3–6 / m² to the standard plastering cost, depending on the method used.

Options for Artex Ceilings

  • Skim over artex (post-1985 or confirmed asbestos-free) — £10–14 / m². Quick, clean and cost-effective. The texture is filled and a smooth finish applied. Suitable where the artex is well bonded.
  • Remove and replaster — £15–22 / m². Artex stripped back to the base, surface prepared, skim applied. Best result but more disruptive and more expensive.
  • Overboard with new plasterboard and skim — £20–30 / m². New 9.5mm board fixed over the artex ceiling and skimmed. Seals the artex entirely. Fast, minimal dust, excellent finish.

How to Compare Plastering Quotes in Sheffield

Collecting two or three quotes is sensible, but the lowest price is not always the best value. Here is what to check before you choose:

  • 01Is it a fixed price or a day rate? Day rates sound low but are open-ended — the job could cost much more if complications arise. Always ask for a fixed price per job.
  • 02Is preparation included? Some plasterers quote for the plaster application only and charge separately for surface preparation, dust sheets, or beading. Our quotes include everything.
  • 03Is it a skim or a full replaster? Make sure every quote is for the same scope of work. A quote for a skim coat will always appear cheaper than one for a full replaster — but these are different jobs with very different results.
  • 04Who does the work? Ask whether the plasterer uses subcontractors. Our work is always done by our own directly employed team, which means consistent quality and a clear line of accountability.
  • 05Do they offer a guarantee? A confident plasterer stands behind their work. We provide a written workmanship guarantee on every job.

Plastering Cost FAQ

Plastering in Sheffield costs between £8 and £28 per square metre in 2026, depending on the type of work. A skim coat finish costs £8–12/m², a full two-coat replaster costs £12–18/m², dry lining and skim costs £18–28/m², and bonding and skim costs £10–15/m². For a typical room, expect to pay £200–700 depending on size.
Plastering a small bedroom (box room) in Sheffield typically costs £200–400. A standard double bedroom costs £300–600. Prices include skim coating all four walls and the ceiling. A full replaster — stripping back to the brick and applying two coats — costs more, typically £400–800 for a double bedroom.
If your existing plaster is sound — not crumbling, blowing or damp-damaged — a skim coat is usually all you need. Skimming applies a thin 2–3mm finishing coat over the existing surface. If the existing plaster is failing, hollow, or heavily damaged, a full replaster is needed. We assess this honestly at the free survey and will never recommend more work than is necessary.
For interior plastering, scaffolding is not required. Standard hop-up platforms and ladders are sufficient for walls and ceilings at normal heights. External rendering is different — scaffolding is required and is always included in our rendering quotes.
Newly plastered walls need to dry fully before painting — typically 2–4 weeks for a skim coat in normal conditions. The colour change from dark pink to pale buff indicates drying progress, but always wait until the surface is fully dry throughout. Always apply a mist coat (50/50 emulsion and water) as the first coat to allow the new plaster to breathe and bond properly.
