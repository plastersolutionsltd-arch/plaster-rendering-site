Why Choose Silicone

The Gold Standard for Sheffield.

Bents Green, Sheffield — completed by P&R Solutions

Silicone render is the gold standard for external wall protection. Here's why Sheffield homeowners choose it over every other system.

Get a Quote ↗
Self-Cleaning, Always Pristine ›

Hydrophobic technology causes rain to sheet off the surface, washing away dirt and algae naturally. No pressure washing, no scrubbing. Most Sheffield properties need attention only once every 3–5 years — if at all. Unlike acrylic which turns green within a few years, silicone stays bright through Sheffield's wet winters.

UV-Stable Colour, 25+ Years ›

Colour runs through the full thickness — nothing to peel, chip, or repaint. UV-stable pigments resist Sheffield's climate for 25+ years. Properties rendered in the early 2000s still look freshly done, with no chalking or fading.

Flexible — Never Cracks ›

Buildings shift with temperature through every freeze-thaw cycle. Silicone resin flexes with that movement — eliminating the hairline cracking that plagues traditional sand-and-cement render. The alkali-resistant mesh reinforcement layer absorbs the stress.

Stops Damp & Moisture ›

Hydrophobic on the outside, breathable on the inside. Moisture vapour escapes while rainwater can't penetrate. Eliminates the trapped damp that causes mould, cold spots, and structural damage — especially important for Sheffield's solid-wall Victorian properties.

The System

What Goes on Your Wall.

Grenoside, Sheffield — 6-layer silicone system. Shortcuts in any layer lead to failure in 5 years. We don't take them.

A professional silicone render is built in 6 distinct layers. Each serves a specific function — together they create a system that lasts 25+ years.

Book Survey ↗
01 Base Coat Foundation
›

Fibre-reinforced polymer-modified base coat applied directly to the substrate. Provides structural strength and ensures proper adhesion across every surface type — brick, blockwork, or existing render.

02 Alkali-Resistant Mesh
›

High-grade reinforcement mesh embedded into the base coat while wet. Allows the whole system to flex with building movement — eliminating the hairline cracking that plagues traditional cement render through Sheffield's freeze-thaw cycles.

03 Levelling Coat
›

Thin levelling coat creates a perfectly smooth, uniform surface — ensuring consistent texture and finish quality across the full elevation. Critical for a professional result on uneven substrates.

04 Professional Primer
›

Professional primer ensures optimal adhesion and colour intensity for the final topcoat. Never skipped — this is what separates a lasting 25-year system from one that fails in five.

05 Silicone Topcoat
›

Premium silicone finish — hydrophobic, self-cleaning, through-coloured. The surface your home will wear for the next 25+ years. UV-stable pigment runs through the full thickness; nothing to peel or repaint.

06 Texture Options
›

Fine, medium, or coarse (typically 1.5mm). Your choice of texture affects water drainage, how light plays across the surface, and the overall aesthetic of the finished property. We'll show you samples at the site survey.

01 Product
What is silicone render and why is it better than cement render?

Silicone render is a premium polymer-modified thin-coat system containing silicone resin. Unlike cement render, silicone is flexible (no cracking), hydrophobic (rain sheets straight off), and breathable. The colour runs throughout the full thickness — nothing to peel, chip, or repaint.

+
02 Cost & Quotes
How much does silicone render cost for a typical Sheffield house?

A typical 100m² detached house costs £7,500–£12,000 including scaffold, prep, and a full professional system. Semi-detached properties range from £4,500–£7,000. We charge £75–£100+ per m² and provide a fully itemised quote after a free survey. No deposit — pay only on completion.

+
03 Warranty
How long does silicone render last?

A professionally installed silicone render carries a 25-year manufacturer product warranty plus our 5-year workmanship guarantee. In practice, on a properly prepared substrate, it will exceed 25 years. Properties rendered in the early 2000s are still performing beautifully.

+
04 Brands
Which brand do you recommend — Ecorend, K Rend, or Baumit?

We recommend Ecorend as our primary choice — exceptional self-cleaning technology and durability. K Rend is excellent value with a wider colour range. Baumit suits older masonry substrates and eco-conscious homeowners. We advise the right system at the free site survey.

+
05 Preparation
Can you render over existing pebbledash?

In most cases, yes. If the pebbledash is sound and well-bonded we apply a reinforcing mesh base coat then the silicone finish over the top. If sections are hollow, cracked, or failing we strip those areas to the masonry first. We always explain our findings before work begins.

+
06 Performance
Will it stay clean in Sheffield's rainy weather?

Sheffield's frequent rainfall works in your favour. Rain sheets off the hydrophobic surface, washing away dirt naturally. Most properties need cleaning only once every 3–5 years — if at all. The self-cleaning technology is specifically designed for the UK climate.

+
07 Value
Is silicone render worth it?

Yes — for most UK homes it's the best long-term investment you can make in your exterior. It eliminates the repainting cycle (every 5–10 years at £2,000–£5,000 a time), protects against water ingress, and adds kerb appeal. Over 25 years the total cost of ownership is substantially lower than painted render or pebbledash upkeep.

+
08 Comparison
What's the difference between silicone render and monocouche?

Monocouche is a through-coloured cement-based product applied in a single thick coat — durable but rigid. Silicone render is a thin-coat polymer system: far more flexible, genuinely hydrophobic, and self-cleaning. Silicone is the premium choice; monocouche is a solid mid-tier option where budget is tighter.

+
09 Maintenance
Do you need to paint silicone render?

No — and this is one of its biggest advantages. The colour is integral throughout the full thickness, pigmented with UV-stable tints. Nothing to peel, chip, or repaint. Unlike masonry paint or sand-and-cement render, silicone maintains its colour and surface integrity for 25+ years with zero repainting.

+
10 Process
How long does it take to apply silicone render?

A typical semi-detached house (80–120m²) takes 3–5 working days from scaffold erection to final coat. A detached property or one with significant prep work may take 5–8 days. We give you a firm programme at the site survey so you know exactly what to expect.
