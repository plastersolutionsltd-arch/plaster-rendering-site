Why Choose Professional Dry Lining

Flat Walls. Fast. First Time.

Sheffield — dry lining installation by P&R Solutions. Clean, level, decoration-ready.

Dry lining is the fastest way to achieve a perfectly flat, smooth wall or ceiling — whether you're boarding a new extension, insulating a cold solid-wall home, or dividing a room with a stud partition.

Get a Quote ↗
Faster Than Traditional Plastering ›

Dry lining eliminates the long drying times associated with traditional wet plaster base coats. Boards are fixed in hours, not days. Once the skim coat is applied and dried (typically 2–4 days), the room is ready for decoration. For extensions, new builds and renovation projects on a programme, dry lining keeps everything on schedule.

Levels Uneven Masonry Walls ›

Older Sheffield properties — terraces, semis and stone-built houses — often have uneven masonry walls that are difficult to plaster to a truly flat finish. Dot and dab dry lining creates a flat, plumb surface regardless of the wall behind. The adhesive dab thickness can be adjusted to bring the board face to a perfect plane, ready for a thin skim coat.

Adds Thermal Insulation ›

Insulated plasterboard — plasterboard with a rigid PIR or EPS insulation layer bonded to the back — creates an effective thermal break when dot and dabbed to cold external walls. In Sheffield's solid-wall Victorian and Edwardian housing stock, this makes a dramatic difference to interior warmth without requiring any external scaffold or render work. Cold walls, condensation and damp patches on internal surfaces are significantly reduced.

Create Rooms With Stud Partitions ›

Metal stud partition walls allow you to divide a room, create an en-suite, add a home office or convert a garage with a solid, skim-finished wall that looks permanent. The metal frame is built to your specification, boarded both sides, taped, jointed and skimmed. We can include door openings, service voids for electrical first fix, and soundproof insulation in the cavity.

5-Year Workmanship Guarantee ›

Every dry lining job is covered by our 5-year workmanship guarantee. If any boarding, jointing or skim fails due to our workmanship within that period, we return and remedy it at no charge. Zero deposit throughout — you pay only when the work is 100% complete and you've inspected it.

How We Work

How We Install Dry Lining.

Sheffield — dry lining installation in progress. The right preparation makes the finish.

Professional dry lining follows 5 key stages. Every stage matters — from layout planning through to the final skim finish.

Book a Survey ↗
1

Survey & Layout Planning

Walls and ceilings measured, method agreed (dot and dab, stud or direct fix), services identified and insulation specification confirmed where required.

2

Frame or Adhesive Preparation

Masonry walls cleaned and checked for plumb; adhesive dabs applied at centres for dot and dab. For stud: metal track fixed to floor, ceiling and side walls; studs inserted at 400mm centres.

3

Plasterboard Installation

Boards cut and fixed — pressed into adhesive dabs or screwed to studs at set centres. Tapered edges aligned for jointing. All cut edges at internal corners.

4

Tape, Joint & Bead

Scrim tape applied to all joints and internal angles. Metal angle beads fixed to external corners. Jointing coat applied, feathered flat and allowed to set before checking for level.

5

Skim Finish & Hand-Over

A 2–3mm finish skim coat applied over the full surface, worked to a perfectly smooth finish. Dry in 2–4 days. Final inspection and sign-off. 5-year guarantee. Zero deposit throughout.

FAQ

Common Questions About Dry Lining

What is dry lining? +

Dry lining means fixing plasterboard to walls or ceilings rather than applying traditional wet plaster to masonry. The boards are then taped, jointed and skimmed for a smooth finish. Faster than traditional plastering and the standard method for new-build partitions, insulated walls and ceiling upgrades.

What is dot and dab plasterboard? +

Dot and dab is the most common dry lining method for masonry walls. Plasterboard adhesive is applied in blobs (dabs) at regular centres across the wall, then the board is pressed firmly into position. The adhesive grips as it cures, creating a flat, plumb surface even on uneven masonry. Boards are then taped and skimmed to a smooth finish.

How much does dry lining cost in Sheffield? +

Dot and dab to a standard room costs from £250. Metal stud partitions from £400 per wall. Ceiling boarding from £200 per room. Insulated plasterboard systems from £350 per room. We provide a free, fully itemised quotation with no obligation and zero deposit required.

What is insulated dry lining and is it worth it? +

Insulated dry lining uses plasterboard with rigid PIR insulation bonded to the back. When dot and dabbed to cold external walls, it creates a thermal break that dramatically reduces heat loss and eliminates cold spots. In Sheffield's solid-wall Victorian and Edwardian terraces, it's one of the most cost-effective interior improvements possible — the difference in room warmth is immediate.

How long does dry lining a room take? +

A standard dot and dab room takes approximately one day — boarding, taping, jointing and skim. Stud partition walls take 1–2 days. The skim finish needs 2–4 days to dry before decorating. We provide a clear programme at quotation stage so you know exactly what to expect.

Can you build stud partition walls? +

Yes — full-height metal stud partitions for room division, en-suites, home offices, dressing rooms and garage conversions. The frame is built from metal track, boarded both sides, taped, jointed and skimmed. We include door openings, service voids for electrical first fix, and soundproof insulation in the cavity.
