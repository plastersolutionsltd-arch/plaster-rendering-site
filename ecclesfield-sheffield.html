Survey & Quote

Ecclesfield is one of north Sheffield's most distinctive areas — a traditional village at its heart, centred on the historic St Mary's Church and the conservation area surrounding it, surrounded by post-war residential estates that grew outward through the 1950s, 1960s and 1970s. This mix of old and new means we work here with two quite different briefs.

For the older stone cottages and Victorian properties in and around the village, the priority is a sympathetic finish — monocouche render is the most popular choice, delivering a through-colour textured coat that complements the stone character of the area rather than clashing with it. Colour matching to neighbouring properties and existing finishes is something we do regularly on Ecclesfield village jobs.

On the wider Ecclesfield estates, the housing stock is predominantly brick-built post-war semis. Many of these properties have never been rendered, and bare ageing brickwork is both visually tired and thermally poor. Silicone render or EWI brings these homes up to a standard that genuinely transforms how they look and feel inside. As neighbours in Grenoside, we are on Ecclesfield doorsteps — no long travel time, no premium for distance.

Ecclesfield FAQ

01 Coverage
Do you work in Ecclesfield village and the surrounding area?
+

Yes — we are based in Grenoside, which is just a few minutes' drive from Ecclesfield. We cover the whole area: the old village around St Mary's Church, Whitley, Potter Hill, Ecclesfield Common and the surrounding residential estates. There is no call-out charge and all surveys are free. We are genuinely local — Ecclesfield is very much on our doorstep.

02 Render Systems
What render suits Ecclesfield's older stone properties?
+

Monocouche render is the traditional and most sympathetic choice for Ecclesfield's older stone cottages and properties near the conservation area. It is a through-colour single-coat render that creates a textured, natural-looking finish — it can be mixed to closely complement the surrounding stone and maintains the historic character of the village far better than a bright smooth silicone coat. For properties in or near the conservation area, we advise on the most appropriate system at survey stage and can discuss any planning considerations that may apply.

03 Estate Housing
Is silicone render suitable for the brick semis on the Ecclesfield estates?
+

Yes — silicone render is an excellent choice for the brick-built semis on Ecclesfield's post-war estates. It bonds securely to existing brickwork after proper preparation, provides a weatherproof hydrophobic surface that self-cleans in rain, and dramatically improves the appearance of standard estate houses. It is available in a huge range of colours so you can choose something that stands out or blends in with the surrounding street. For a 1960s or 1970s semi in Ecclesfield, silicone render is typically the most cost-effective external upgrade available.

04 Colour Matching
Can you match the colour of existing render on an Ecclesfield cottage?
+

Yes — colour matching is something we do regularly on Ecclesfield's older properties. For a partial repair or extension to an existing rendered surface, we take a reference from the existing finish and work with our suppliers to find the closest available match. Where an entire property is being stripped and re-rendered, the new colour is entirely your choice from hundreds of shades across silicone and monocouche ranges. We always bring colour chips to the survey so you can see how options look against your property in natural light before committing.

05 Insulation
Do you offer EWI for older Ecclesfield properties?
+

Yes — EWI is available for a wide range of Ecclesfield property types, including solid-wall stone and brick cottages as well as post-war cavity-wall semis. For solid-wall properties in particular, EWI is one of the only practical ways to significantly improve thermal performance without major internal disruption. The insulation board is fixed to the exterior wall and finished with a durable render — the external finish can be selected to maintain the character of the property. We survey every property thoroughly before recommending any EWI system and will advise honestly on whether it is the right solution for your construction type and situation.
